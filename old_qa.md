It's complementary, not a replacement. CRXcavator tells you *what permissions it has*, ManifestGuard tells you *what it's actually doing with them*.\n\n### Q2: \"How do you handle zero-day extensions that aren't in your threat intel database?\"\n> **Answer:** That's exactly why I built the dual-scoring engine instead of just a blocklist. The threat intel DB is a fast lane for known-bad, but the behavioral engine is the safety net for unknown threats.\n\n### Q3: \"What's your false positive rate?\"\n> **Answer:** Honestly, the initial version had a high false positive rate — legitimate tools like password managers and ad blockers were flagged simply because they need broad permissions.
